‚ùìProblem framing

üí°

This section outlines the problem that technology-assisted review (TAR) is trying to solve and provides context on existing functionalities before the Active Learning feature.

The corpus of documents in legal review is usually massive. Traditionally, lawyers uses paper-based, linear document review methods (e.g. reviewing all 100k documents) to find relevant documents for their case, which is extremely time-consuming.

Everlaw offers a Predictive Coding (PC) tool that utilizes supervised learning to streamline document review. This tool helps users focus on important documents and reduces manual review workload to achieve review targets efficiently. A PC model can be trained after users have identified 100 relevant documents. The PC model then assesses all project documents, scoring them based on contextual similarity with the training set, aiding review prioritization. Users have reported that they are able to get 60% more relevant documents based on PC while reviewing 30% less documents than traditional review methods that are based on keyword searches.

However, there is disconnection in integrating PC models into team-based legal review processes. Review teams usually have more than 5 members, and coordinating the distribution of highly predicted documents among multiple team members can be challenging, leading to potential inefficiencies in the review workflow.

üë§¬†User insights and pain points

Sources: user interviews and usability tests with users from 8 organizations.

  • There are large confusions around how to use PC model predictions and immediate next action items after users have received predicted relevance of each document. Users need guidance at the beginning of PC model creation on what to review and how to fine tune the model
  • Users want to quickly get started on reviewing documents directly from the PC page after the model has generated prediction scores. They want to set up a review workflow leveraging PC with minimal effort of balancing the review of relevant documents and documents to improve the model.
  • Users are not aware of means they can improve the model prediction (reduce bias)
  • Reviewers work in teams. Therefore, an assignment mechanism that can distribute documents among reviewers is crucial. Reviewers wants to know the progress of review, i.e. see what percentage of documents they have finished reviewing out of the total documents that they have been assigned to.
  • After the model has generated prediction scores, the pool of documents that users care the most about are (in descending order of priority): highly predicted documents, conflicts, and low coverage.
  • Users prefer to keep document families (email threads and attachments) together when reviewing documents.

üéØ¬†Goal setting

  • We hope to offload the decision-making (i.e. what document to review) burden on users by using providing a centralized assignment queue that automatically recommends documents that are the ‚Äúbest-suited‚Äù to be reviewed based on the current state of the model.
  • The core value of this feature is that users don‚Äôt need to think about balancing the review between predicted-relevant documents (with highest prediction scores) and documents that can improve the model, by presenting users with the next ‚Äúbest‚Äù document to review.
  • The queue will do the job of determining a mix of documents that will allow users to review highly predicted documents as well as documents that can improve their model, so users can find relevant documents faster over time.

üíº¬†Feature scope

To offer a complete Active Learning (AL) experience, this feature will make the following changes:

  • Build a single Active Learning ‚Äúassignment queue‚Äù that users can leverage with PC to aid team review distribution
  • Improve model update cadence with automatic updates after a certain number of documents have been reviewed
  • Determine the order of documents in the queue (after the model has prediction scores) to balance highly predicted documents, low coverage documents, and documents from random, which can ensure both highly predicted documents are reviewed and the model is continuously well trained with addressing documents from low coverage.

üé®¬†Design summary

Active Learning assignment queue development

We will add a dynamic queue for PC that determines the order of all eligible documents in the project in a descending order of their values (relevance and helpfulness in improving the model). The order will also be constantly updated as more reviewed documents are fed back to the model for training. Any reviewer is free to grab documents from the queue, with the confidence that the next document they grab from the queue will be the ‚Äúnext best‚Äù one.

üí≠

Discussion point: when should the AL learning queue be available? Keep in mind that the PC model only generates prediction scores after 100 relevant documents are identified.

Option 1) Make the queue only available after the model has prediction scores

From user interviews, we learnt that before PC models generate initial prediction scores, project admins prefer to first find documents via targeted searches to meet the document threshold to generate prediction scores, instead of reviewing at random because the richness of relevant documents might be very low. This means that before the model has prediction scores, we have limited information to identify relevant documents for the queue.

Option 2) It will be available right after model creation

  • This option offer the complete Active Learning experience for them.
  • Users can still review in their own way if they prefer, but the queue will an option there is they don‚Äôt know where to start
  • In the edge case where users find 0 relevant documents after reviewing 100 documents, give a warning to recommend targeted search .

Final decision and action items:

  • Offer the AL queue at the beginning of model creation, as we learnt a big challenge for a lot of clients is that they don‚Äôt know what to do at the beginning from model creation
  • Lowering the threshold of PC scores generation to 50 to accelerate the process.
Automatic model updates

We want to provide users with a smooth Active Learning experience with the goal to minimize user‚Äôs efforts in managing the PC model. We can automatically queue the model for update after users have finished reviewing a certain number of documents.

It is worth noting that update frequency matters more in the early stages of the model. For example, so we should adjust the model update frequency disproportionately to the number of documents that have been used to train the model.

Order of documents in queue

We will use the Epsilon Greedy Algorithm with scheduled decays to manage the ratio between highly predicted documents and documents from low coverage. The algorithm focuses on the dynamic combination between ‚Äúexploration and exploitation‚Äù and essentially, we will be pulling from three pools of documents:

  • Documents with the highest prediction scores
  • Random sample from low coverage documents
  • Truly random documents
Document families and best practices

Document families refer to email threads and attachments. We should explicitly communicate to users both in-platform and in KB that we recommend the following coding practice for document families to maximize model training accuracy:

Each document‚Äôs relevance should only be determined by its content, not its relation with a family, as it will negatively impact the model if we code family members as relevant only because of its relation to another highly predicted document in the family.

‚è≥¬†Expected user workflows

  1. Users set up a PC model and set the criteria for relevant documents.
  2. The assignment queue contains all PC-eligible, unreviewed documents in the project, but orders them based on their value (both prediction scores and helpfulness in improving the model), which also changes constantly with model updates as more documents are reviewed.
  3. Users can assign any number of documents to reviewers from the queue.
  4. Before the model has prediction scores, the order of documents in the queue will be random. After the model has prediction scores, documents in the queue will be a combination of highly predicted documents, a random sample of low coverage documents, and truly random documents, with their ratio adjusted by the Epsilon Greedy model.
  5. The PC model will automatically be queued for an update after a certain number of documents have been reviewed. After each model update, the order of documents in the queue will be changed, and therefore, the order of unassigned documents in the assignment group will be changed as well. Documents that have already been assigned out will not be changed.
  6. Reviewers will also be able to track their review progress on a PC dashboard.
