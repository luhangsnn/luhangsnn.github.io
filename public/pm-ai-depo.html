AI Deposition Analyzer

PM UX Research Prompt Engineering

Product Overview: Storybuilder by Everlaw allows legal professional to collaborate in drafting depositions, upload and sync deposition videos and transcripts, and cite and link documents and testimonies in the collaborative editor.

This is a high-priority feature that has a tight release deadline within 2 months due to user-facing marketing.

‚ùìProblem framing

Deposition transcripts are very long, making the task of summarization and locating crucial information within them highly laborious. For example, a 200-page deposition transcript can take a paralegal around 8 hours to summarize. These transcripts summarization tasks also have quick turnaround requirements: after the transcript is released after a deposition, a group paralegals usually only have a couple of hours to summarize the segment that they are assigned to before handing it over the the attorneys, who need to correlate the summaries with the original testimonies within the same day to prepare for examination the next day.

In March 2023, Everlaw released a Beta version of using Generative-AI features to assist legal writing in Storybuilder. My responsibility focuses on working with Beta users and continuously shipping AI features based on iterative feedback. Generative AI has shown the potential to generate transcript summaries within minutes.

üìå¬†Key decisions

Originally, we planned to focus on offering the most commonly used summary format - page/line summaries - using LLM. However, it became apparent during my intensive user research that the value for an AI-generated page/line summary lies deep in a navigable UI. Due to the tight release deadline, for the initial version of this feature we do not want to invest in complex UI right away, but want to experiment with user receptivity of AI generated summaries.

My learnings from user research reflected that the most value-add for users lies in providing analytical summaries where users can customize their summary reports out of a set of options as their work product.

Goals for users:

We want to offload users‚Äô burden of summarizing and analyzing lengthy transcripts in short turnaround time after a deposition and satisfy their various transcript summaries needs by providing them with options to build a deposition analysis with sections that they desire. We want to offer users the maximum flexibility to experiment with LLM‚Äôs ability to analyze depositions.

üíº¬†Feature scope

Users can choose to generate the following LLM tasks in Storybuilder. Each task involves a specific prompting strategy with the LLM:

  • Full case summary: offer users a comprehensive, broad overview of the deposition, while summaries for each section should be relatively concise.
  • Summary by topics
  • Summary by key witness: this type of summaries focus specifically on the testimony of key witnesses involved in the deposition, where we provide overviews of their background, statements, responses to questions, and any crucial details they provided during the deposition
  • Discrepancy analysis: LLM will extract factual statements made in depositions and examine inconsistencies.
  • Sentiment analysis: LLM will analyze the sentiment of key testimonies and access the overall tone, biases, and emotions of the witness
  • Custom analysis task, which will include a free form text field for users to type in their customized instructions.
