Data Subject Access Request¬†(DSAR) gives an individual the right to request information about the way companies handle their personal information. It helps individuals to understand how and why organizations are using their personal data, and check if they are doing it in a lawful manner. This practice is widely used in the UK and EU.

Individuals have the right to obtain:

  • confirmation that an organization is processing their personal data;
  • together with a copy of this personal data; and

Organizations are only required to disclose information from a document that pertains to the specific individual, together with the document ID and filename.

Traditional DSAR workflow is conducted through mass redaction. Users will first find the documents that contain the requestor‚Äôs information, such as name, email, and phone number, through key word searches, then redact the entire document while only leaving out the requestor‚Äôs personal information. Finally, the redacted document is sent to the requestor.

There are several problems with this approach:

  • It is time-consuming to redact a huge amount for information, as personal information of the requestor usually only occupies a single line in a document.
  • This approach bears the risk of under-react easily ‚Äî accidentally leaving out other individual‚Äôs information on the same document, which could introduce data leaks.
  • It is also risky to disclose original documents with redactions, as there might be sensitive information in the document‚Äôs metadata that can easily be ignored during review.

‚úÖ¬†Improved workflow

To address the above pain points, we suggest users to meet the DSAR requirements by only disclosing relevant personal data from a document, instead of mass redacting the irrelevant portion, which is usually the majority. Highlighting the portions that are relevant is much faster and less risky than redacting huge amounts of information, plus it's advantageous for clients to withhold entire documents when possible.

Our suggested improved workflow is to highlight the relevant information in documents in batch through keyword searches and export the highlighted text to a spreadsheet with document ID and filename.

üíé¬†Feature scope and design

To facilitate the improved user workflow, we need the following new functionalities.

Apply text highlights to documents in batch

Users should be able to search for keywords and information patterns (e.g. emails, phone numbers, SSN, credit card number) across all documents and apply highlights to all search hits at once. This is delivered by a ‚Äúbatch highlight‚Äù functionality at the document overview center.

Extract highlighted text from documents to spreadsheets in batch

In the document overview center, we added an option for users to batch export highlighted text from all documents into a single spreadsheet. The spreadsheet is primarily a information table that has columns for the document ID, file name, and all highlighted text in the same row for a single document.

Secure way to deliver final documents

The final step to complete the DSAR workflow for organizations is to help them securely deliver the information spreadsheet to the requestor. For any exported files, we added the option to share through internal transfers or sharable links.
